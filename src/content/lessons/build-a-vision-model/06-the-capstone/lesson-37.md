Every accuracy figure through chapter 5 and lesson 36 was measured on the same eight samples the network trained on - a fair check that training worked, but not a test of anything the network had to figure out on its own. `GenerateSample(8)` gives you that test: the same formula from lesson 29, run one step further than the dataset ever went, producing a vertical edge at column 5 when training only ever showed the network columns 1 through 4.

The network gets it right - 0.742 favours the correct class, comfortably over the 0.5 line. But it is not the confident 0.99-and-climbing you might expect from a network that hit 100 percent accuracy in lesson 36. That gap is not a flaw in today's numbers; it is the whole point of testing on unseen data. A network only ever gets pushed toward confidence on the exact positions it is shown, and column 5 was never one of them. A lower, honest margin on a genuinely new input is more informative than a suspiciously perfect one would have been.
