Lesson 14 built one pulse as a function of phase. Today you repeat it. The
**glottal source** is that single pulse played back at a fixed rate - the
**fundamental frequency**, F0 - so all this lesson adds is a **phase
accumulator**: a running fraction that steps forward by `F0/SampleRate` every
sample and wraps back around at `1.0`, handing lesson 14's `PulseShape` a fresh
phase each time.

100 Hz at a sample rate of 16000 gives a period of exactly 160 samples, with no
remainder, which is exactly why those two numbers were chosen: the spec can
assert that `pulse[160]` matches `pulse[0]` bit for bit, instead of tracking a
fractional period that would drift over time. `pulse[96]` is the peak, because
sample 96 is phase 0.6 - the open quotient, where lesson 14's pulse reached
`1.0`. The same accumulator handles the untidy, drifting periods that lesson
18's pitch contour will eventually feed it.
